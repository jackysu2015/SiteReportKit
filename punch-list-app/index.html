The operational problem

The result is a client-ready PDF, a focused contractor action list, a sortable CSV register, or a local project archive—not an unstructured camera roll.

A useful punch list has to do more than store photos. It must tell the recipient what is wrong, where it is, who owns it, when it is due, and whether the correction has been accepted. SiteReportKit keeps those details with the issue from first capture through closeout.

Who this is for: General contractors, owners’ representatives, architects, inspectors, developers, and specialty contractors who need a clear record after a walkthrough.

What a reliable field record needs

Every item should be understandable without relying on the inspector’s memory. Keep the description factual, make the location findable, show enough photographic context, and write the required action as an outcome that can be checked. Responsibility and due dates make the register actionable; status and closeout evidence make it traceable.

Field and report checklist

Adapt the checklist to your role, contract, jurisdiction, records policy, and the technical scope of the visit. SiteReportKit organises information; it does not confer inspection authority or make professional judgments for the user.

  • Offline issue capture
  • Photo annotation and captions
  • Assignee, trade, severity, status, and due date
  • PDF report and scoped action list
  • CSV issue register
  • After photos and resolution notes

Report quality checks before issue

  1. Confirm the title, author, date, site, client, scope, and intended recipient.
  2. Check that item references are unique and locations use one consistent hierarchy.
  3. Remove duplicates and split conditions that require different owners or closeout decisions.
  4. Verify captions, annotations, names, dates, status, and required actions against the field record.
  5. Open the exported PDF, XLSX, or CSV, inspect image legibility, page breaks, filters, and counts, then store the issued version under the project’s records process.

Frequently asked questions

Questions about punch list app

Can I use SiteReportKit without an account?

Yes. SiteReportKit is designed for local, offline-first capture and does not require an account to start a project.

What can I export?

You can create professional PDF reports, contractor action lists, CSV issue registers, closeout reports, and local project archives.

Is a punch list the same as a snag list?

They describe closely related workflows. “Punch list” is common in the United States, while “snag list” and “snagging” are widely used in the United Kingdom and other markets.

Can I record completed work?

Yes. Add resolution notes and after photos so the final closeout record shows both the original observation and corrective evidence.
