Closeout guide

A construction closeout checklist that proves more than “complete.”

Use stable item references, before-and-after evidence, resolution notes, and explicit review states to turn an outstanding-work list into a final record.

The operational problem

A structured closeout pack helps the next reader understand the original condition, the response, the evidence, unresolved exceptions, and the final status at issue date.

Marking a row complete does not show what changed or who accepted it. Closeout should preserve the original issue, record the claimed correction, and distinguish contractor completion from reviewer acceptance where the project requires it.

Who this is for: Contractors, project managers, owner representatives, design teams, quality managers, and handover coordinators closing punch, snag, or defect registers.

What a reliable field record needs

Every item should be understandable without relying on the inspector’s memory. Keep the description factual, make the location findable, show enough photographic context, and write the required action as an outcome that can be checked. Responsibility and due dates make the register actionable; status and closeout evidence make it traceable.

A three-stage workflow

01

Prepare

Freeze or reference the issued action list so item identifiers remain stable.

02

Capture and review

Collect resolution notes and after evidence, then review against the stated required action.

03

Issue and close

Reopen inadequate work, record acceptance appropriately, issue the final report, and retain the source project and exports.

Field and report checklist

Adapt the checklist to your role, contract, jurisdiction, records policy, and the technical scope of the visit. SiteReportKit organises information; it does not confer inspection authority or make professional judgments for the user.

  • Stable source item reference
  • Original evidence retained
  • Correction description
  • After photo with useful context
  • Reviewer state and acceptance note
  • Final open-item exceptions

Report quality checks before issue

  1. Confirm the title, author, date, site, client, scope, and intended recipient.
  2. Check that item references are unique and locations use one consistent hierarchy.
  3. Remove duplicates and split conditions that require different owners or closeout decisions.
  4. Verify captions, annotations, names, dates, status, and required actions against the field record.
  5. Open the exported PDF, XLSX, or CSV, inspect image legibility, page breaks, filters, and counts, then store the issued version under the project’s records process.

Frequently asked questions

Questions about closeout guide

Is contractor-complete the same as accepted?

Not always. Many projects require a separate review or acceptance step. Model the statuses to match your actual process.

Should original photos remain in the final report?

Keeping before evidence makes the closeout record easier to understand and audit alongside the after evidence.

What if the correction is incomplete?

Keep or return the item to an open state, explain what remains, and request new evidence or another visit as the project process requires.

What files should be retained?

Retain the final issued reports and, where appropriate, the underlying project archive and CSV register under your organisation’s records policy.
