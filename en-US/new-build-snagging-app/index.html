The operational problem

Generate a polished handover report for the client and a separate action list that keeps contractors focused on outstanding work.

New-build handover produces many small observations across repeated rooms and finishes. Consistent areas, trade labels, photographs, and item references help the project team distinguish, allocate, and close them efficiently.

Who this is for: Home inspectors, developers, purchasers’ representatives, residential contractors, fit-out teams, and property professionals documenting handover condition.

What a reliable field record needs

Every item should be understandable without relying on the inspector’s memory. Keep the description factual, make the location findable, show enough photographic context, and write the required action as an outcome that can be checked. Responsibility and due dates make the register actionable; status and closeout evidence make it traceable.

Field and report checklist

Adapt the checklist to your role, contract, jurisdiction, records policy, and the technical scope of the visit. SiteReportKit organises information; it does not confer inspection authority or make professional judgments for the user.

  • Room-by-room structure
  • Finish and trade categories
  • Annotated before photos
  • Client change distinction
  • Outstanding snag action list
  • After photos and acceptance notes

Report quality checks before issue

  1. Confirm the title, author, date, site, client, scope, and intended recipient.
  2. Check that item references are unique and locations use one consistent hierarchy.
  3. Remove duplicates and split conditions that require different owners or closeout decisions.
  4. Verify captions, annotations, names, dates, status, and required actions against the field record.
  5. Open the exported PDF, XLSX, or CSV, inspect image legibility, page breaks, filters, and counts, then store the issued version under the project’s records process.

Frequently asked questions

Questions about new-build snagging

Is SiteReportKit a substitute for a qualified building inspection?

No. It is a documentation and reporting tool. Users should obtain appropriately qualified advice for technical, contractual, or safety judgments.

Can I distinguish client changes from defects?

The project’s categorisation and description fields can be used to keep different observation types clear.

Can I make a handover report and a trade list?

Yes. Both can be generated from the same structured project data.

Can I retain a copy of the project?

Yes. Local project archive export supports controlled transfer and retention within your own file process.
