The operational problem

Evaluate real interior handover and active-construction examples rather than relying on a fictional mock report.

A product screenshot cannot prove whether a report is clear across ten pages, whether item references stay consistent, or whether a trade list is usable. Downloading the complete file is the more useful evaluation.

Who this is for: Inspectors, contractors, consultants, developers, and project teams comparing output quality before adopting a reporting tool.

What a reliable field record needs

Every item should be understandable without relying on the inspector’s memory. Keep the description factual, make the location findable, show enough photographic context, and write the required action as an outcome that can be checked. Responsibility and due dates make the register actionable; status and closeout evidence make it traceable.

Report quality checks before issue

  1. Confirm the title, author, date, site, client, scope, and intended recipient.
  2. Check that item references are unique and locations use one consistent hierarchy.
  3. Remove duplicates and split conditions that require different owners or closeout decisions.
  4. Verify captions, annotations, names, dates, status, and required actions against the field record.
  5. Open the exported PDF, XLSX, or CSV, inspect image legibility, page breaks, filters, and counts, then store the issued version under the project’s records process.

Frequently asked questions

Questions about sample report library

Are these real SiteReportKit outputs?

Yes. The downloadable files are complete sample outputs prepared to demonstrate the reporting structure and visual quality.

Are the sample projects real client projects?

No. The examples use prepared sample project data so they can be distributed publicly without exposing a client record.

Can I download CSV as well as PDF?

Yes. The website provides sample issue registers alongside the PDF reports and action lists.

Why are interior and construction samples separate?

They demonstrate distinct evidence sets, terminology, trades, issue types, and report outputs rather than blending unrelated work into one example.
