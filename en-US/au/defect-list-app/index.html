The operational problem

Create a traceable record with photos, locations, trades, owners, severity, status, due dates, and closeout evidence while applying your own approved project procedure.

Defect terminology, notice requirements, inspection authority, and retention duties differ across contracts, organisations, states, territories, and building classes. The app provides structured documentation, not jurisdiction-specific legal advice.

Who this is for: Australian builders, superintendents, consultants, developers, owners’ representatives, inspectors, and subcontractors managing quality observations and handover work.

What a reliable field record needs

Every item should be understandable without relying on the inspector’s memory. Keep the description factual, make the location findable, show enough photographic context, and write the required action as an outcome that can be checked. Responsibility and due dates make the register actionable; status and closeout evidence make it traceable.

Field and report checklist

Adapt the checklist to your role, contract, jurisdiction, records policy, and the technical scope of the visit. SiteReportKit organises information; it does not confer inspection authority or make professional judgments for the user.

  • A4 presentation
  • Area, element, and trade
  • Annotated photographic evidence
  • Required action and responsibility
  • Due date and status
  • Closeout evidence

Report quality checks before issue

  1. Confirm the title, author, date, site, client, scope, and intended recipient.
  2. Check that item references are unique and locations use one consistent hierarchy.
  3. Remove duplicates and split conditions that require different owners or closeout decisions.
  4. Verify captions, annotations, names, dates, status, and required actions against the field record.
  5. Open the exported PDF, XLSX, or CSV, inspect image legibility, page breaks, filters, and counts, then store the issued version under the project’s records process.

Frequently asked questions

Questions about australia

Does SiteReportKit certify NCC compliance?

No. The app does not certify compliance with the National Construction Code or any Australian Standard. Technical conclusions must come from appropriately qualified people.

Can it be used by a superintendent?

It can support field documentation, but users must follow the powers, notices, and processes established by their appointment and contract.

Can I export data to a spreadsheet workflow?

Yes. CSV issue-register export supports sorting and coordination in other tools.

Does it work offline?

Yes. The field workflow is designed for local, offline-first capture.
