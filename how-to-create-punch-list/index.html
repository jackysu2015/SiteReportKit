Field workflow guide

How to create a punch list people can actually close.

The quality of a punch list is decided during capture: precise locations, one issue per item, useful evidence, and an action that can be verified.

The operational problem

A disciplined workflow produces faster allocation, fewer clarification calls, and a final record that shows what was identified and how it was resolved.

Teams often optimise for recording speed but pay for ambiguity later. Combining unrelated defects, omitting room details, or writing “fix this” pushes interpretation onto the recipient and weakens closeout.

Who this is for: First-time inspectors, project engineers, construction managers, owner representatives, and teams replacing ad hoc notes with a repeatable walkthrough process.

What a reliable field record needs

Every item should be understandable without relying on the inspector’s memory. Keep the description factual, make the location findable, show enough photographic context, and write the required action as an outcome that can be checked. Responsibility and due dates make the register actionable; status and closeout evidence make it traceable.

A three-stage workflow

01

Prepare

Plan the route, scope, naming convention, categories, and required recipients before the walk.

02

Capture and review

Create one actionable item per condition with a precise location, concise description, photo, and required outcome.

03

Issue and close

Quality-check, allocate, issue, revisit, record after evidence, and close only when the project’s acceptance process is satisfied.

Field and report checklist

Adapt the checklist to your role, contract, jurisdiction, records policy, and the technical scope of the visit. SiteReportKit organises information; it does not confer inspection authority or make professional judgments for the user.

  • Scope and inspection date
  • Consistent location hierarchy
  • One condition per item
  • Context and close-up photos
  • Specific required outcome
  • Owner, due date, status, and closeout proof

Report quality checks before issue

  1. Confirm the title, author, date, site, client, scope, and intended recipient.
  2. Check that item references are unique and locations use one consistent hierarchy.
  3. Remove duplicates and split conditions that require different owners or closeout decisions.
  4. Verify captions, annotations, names, dates, status, and required actions against the field record.
  5. Open the exported PDF, XLSX, or CSV, inspect image legibility, page breaks, filters, and counts, then store the issued version under the project’s records process.

Frequently asked questions

Questions about field workflow guide

How detailed should a punch item be?

Detailed enough that the recipient can locate the condition, understand the expected outcome, and provide verifiable correction without guessing.

Should I put several issues in one item?

Usually no. Separate items are easier to assign, price, filter, verify, and close independently.

How many photos should an item include?

Use enough to establish context and detail. A wider location view plus a close-up is often more useful than several near-identical images.

When should an item be marked closed?

Only after the required work and evidence meet the project’s agreed review and acceptance process.
