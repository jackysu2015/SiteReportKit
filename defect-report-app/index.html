The operational problem

Export construction evidence as a structured defect report, an outstanding action list, or a CSV register for downstream coordination.

A defect report should be precise enough to support coordination without pretending to replace professional judgment. SiteReportKit provides a consistent evidence record while the inspector remains responsible for classification and required action.

Who this is for: Site inspectors, quality teams, construction managers, design consultants, owner representatives, and contractors documenting active works.

What a reliable field record needs

Every item should be understandable without relying on the inspector’s memory. Keep the description factual, make the location findable, show enough photographic context, and write the required action as an outcome that can be checked. Responsibility and due dates make the register actionable; status and closeout evidence make it traceable.

Field and report checklist

Adapt the checklist to your role, contract, jurisdiction, records policy, and the technical scope of the visit. SiteReportKit organises information; it does not confer inspection authority or make professional judgments for the user.

  • Construction-specific issue register
  • Severity and status fields
  • Annotated evidence photos
  • Required action and owner
  • Outstanding-work PDF
  • Closeout evidence page

Report quality checks before issue

  1. Confirm the title, author, date, site, client, scope, and intended recipient.
  2. Check that item references are unique and locations use one consistent hierarchy.
  3. Remove duplicates and split conditions that require different owners or closeout decisions.
  4. Verify captions, annotations, names, dates, status, and required actions against the field record.
  5. Open the exported PDF, XLSX, or CSV, inspect image legibility, page breaks, filters, and counts, then store the issued version under the project’s records process.

Frequently asked questions

Questions about defect reporting

Does SiteReportKit decide whether work is compliant?

No. The app records observations and evidence; technical and compliance judgments remain with appropriately qualified project professionals.

Can I export open defects only?

Yes. Filter the register and create a focused action list for outstanding work.

Can I separate construction and interior projects?

Yes. Each project keeps its own issue set, evidence, action list, and report outputs.

Is CSV available?

Yes. A CSV issue register can support sorting, coordination, and import into other workflows.
