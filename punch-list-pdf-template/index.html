The operational problem

The sample demonstrates a cover, summary, issue index, evidence pages, action ownership, and closeout structure generated from one project record.

A blank template can standardise headings, but it cannot keep dozens of photos, assignees, due dates, and status changes aligned by itself. The better workflow is to structure each item during capture and generate the presentation at export.

Who this is for: Teams evaluating report formats, replacing Word or spreadsheet templates, or setting a consistent punch list standard across site visits.

What a reliable field record needs

Every item should be understandable without relying on the inspector’s memory. Keep the description factual, make the location findable, show enough photographic context, and write the required action as an outcome that can be checked. Responsibility and due dates make the register actionable; status and closeout evidence make it traceable.

Field and report checklist

Adapt the checklist to your role, contract, jurisdiction, records policy, and the technical scope of the visit. SiteReportKit organises information; it does not confer inspection authority or make professional judgments for the user.

  • Project and client identity
  • Unique item reference
  • Area and precise location
  • Description and required action
  • Annotated photo evidence
  • Owner, priority, due date, and status

Report quality checks before issue

  1. Confirm the title, author, date, site, client, scope, and intended recipient.
  2. Check that item references are unique and locations use one consistent hierarchy.
  3. Remove duplicates and split conditions that require different owners or closeout decisions.
  4. Verify captions, annotations, names, dates, status, and required actions against the field record.
  5. Open the exported PDF, XLSX, or CSV, inspect image legibility, page breaks, filters, and counts, then store the issued version under the project’s records process.

Frequently asked questions

Questions about pdf template and sample

Is the PDF sample a real SiteReportKit output?

Yes. The linked sample is a downloadable report output used to demonstrate the product’s reporting structure.

Can I edit the PDF like a blank form?

The sample is intended as an output reference. SiteReportKit structures the items in the project and generates the PDF from those records.

Should the client report and trade list be identical?

Usually not. A client report benefits from context and presentation, while a trade action list should focus on the outstanding work that recipient needs to complete.

What paper sizes are supported?

Project report settings include paper-size selection so the output can fit the intended market and recipient.
