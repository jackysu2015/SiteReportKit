The operational problem

Complete the inspection on the device, review the record, and choose when to export or share the resulting file.

A field tool that depends on a live connection creates gaps precisely where documentation matters. Offline-first capture keeps the project, issues, photos, markup, and assignments available locally during the walk.

Who this is for: Inspectors and project teams working in low-signal sites, remote locations, secure environments, or projects where account setup and cloud administration add friction.

What a reliable field record needs

Every item should be understandable without relying on the inspector’s memory. Keep the description factual, make the location findable, show enough photographic context, and write the required action as an outcome that can be checked. Responsibility and due dates make the register actionable; status and closeout evidence make it traceable.

Field and report checklist

Adapt the checklist to your role, contract, jurisdiction, records policy, and the technical scope of the visit. SiteReportKit organises information; it does not confer inspection authority or make professional judgments for the user.

  • No account required to begin
  • Local project capture
  • Offline photos and markup
  • Local search, sort, and review
  • Export when ready
  • Project archive for controlled transfer

Report quality checks before issue

  1. Confirm the title, author, date, site, client, scope, and intended recipient.
  2. Check that item references are unique and locations use one consistent hierarchy.
  3. Remove duplicates and split conditions that require different owners or closeout decisions.
  4. Verify captions, annotations, names, dates, status, and required actions against the field record.
  5. Open the exported PDF, XLSX, or CSV, inspect image legibility, page breaks, filters, and counts, then store the issued version under the project’s records process.

Frequently asked questions

Questions about offline-first reporting

Does offline-first mean nothing can be shared?

No. It means capture does not depend on a live connection. You can export and share files when you choose and have a suitable connection or transfer method.

Where is project information kept?

The product uses a local-first project workflow. Your device and your own file-handling process remain important parts of data management.

Can I annotate photos offline?

Yes. Photo evidence and annotation are part of the local field capture workflow.

Do I need to create an organization account?

No account is required to begin using the core local workflow.
